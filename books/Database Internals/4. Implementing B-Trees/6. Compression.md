Databases often use fast compression algorithms (e.g. LZ4, Snappy) to reduce storage and I/O at the cost of some CPU time for compression and decompression.  
Compressed Page -> Read -> Decompress -> Use  
Once loaded, the page is typically kept in memory in decompressed form and can be accessed repeatedly without further decompression. If the page is modified, it may be recompressed before being flushed back to disk.  
A compressed page may occupy less space than the underlying storage transfer unit (e.g. a sector/LBA/block exposed by the device). Reading the compressed page may therefore also retrieve unrelated bytes from the surrounding storage unit.
