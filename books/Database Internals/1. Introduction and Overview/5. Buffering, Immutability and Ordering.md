Storage engines have 3 commonalities  
**Buffering**  
Some databases choose to use additional (avoidable) in-memory buffering to organise and amortize I/O costs while others minimise such buffering.  
**Mutability**  
Some databases choose to update existing files while others choose immutability through append-only (or copy-on-write) operations.  
**Ordering**  
Some databases may store data in key-sorted order to enable efficient range scans while others in insertion order.
